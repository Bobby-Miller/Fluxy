# Contributing To Fluxy

Contributions are welcome after the contribution agreement process is operational.

## License Boundaries

- Python client, WebDev transport, and related files are accepted under the repository's MIT license.
- Gateway module files under `ignition-module/` are accepted under MPL-2.0.
- All contributions require sufficient copyright and patent grants to Green Pipe Partners, LLC to maintain, distribute, and relicense Fluxy.

## CLA Requirement

Do not submit or merge an external contribution until Green Pipe Partners has recorded an approved CLA for the contributor. Individuals use the individual CLA. Contributors acting within employment or on behalf of an organization also require an authorized corporate CLA or documented employer authorization.

The forms under `legal/cla/` are drafts pending counsel approval. Until counsel approves the forms and Green Pipe establishes a signature/recording process, external pull requests may be discussed but must not be merged.

Company founders, employees, and contractors must also have a continuing written agreement covering contributions created after any one-time assignment's effective date.

## Contribution Rules

- Identify third-party code, generated code, and sources in the pull request.
- Do not submit material you lack authority to license.
- Preserve SPDX, copyright, license, and notice files.
- Never submit API credentials, signing material, activation credentials, customer data, or proprietary Ignition resources.
- Add tests for behavior changes and update the applicable MIT or MPL documentation.
- Use Developer Certificate of Origin sign-off only if Green Pipe formally enables it; DCO sign-off does not replace the required CLA.

See `LICENSING.md`, `SECURITY.md`, and `SUPPORT.md` before contributing.
